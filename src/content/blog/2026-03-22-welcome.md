The homepage of this blog is a manifesto.

That is not a design quirk. It is a commitment. The manifesto — *Events All the Way Down: A Process View of Becoming* — is the center of gravity for everything that will appear here. It argues that what we call "things" are not substances but processes, not nouns but verbs, not objects but events in a continuous sequence of readings.

The posts are what happens when that argument refuses to stay abstract.

Some posts will be technical — about how autoregressive language models are, structurally, the most literal implementation of the event-ontology thesis. Some will be about specific thinkers: what Whitehead got right, what Nāgārjuna was actually saying, why Heraclitus is underrated. Some will be about building things — about what it means to architect software when your ontology says there are no stable objects.

Some will be personal. This blog has a protagonist. He lives in Porto Velho, Rondônia. He codes late at night. He writes philosophical essays that nobody asked for. He is trying to figure out whether the systems he builds are a continuation of something the universe was already doing, or whether he is just projecting.

The manifesto is the thesis. The posts are the attempt to live inside it.

*Franklin Silveira Baldo — Porto Velho, March 2026*
